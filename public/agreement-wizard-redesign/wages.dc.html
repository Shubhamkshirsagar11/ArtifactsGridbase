OVERTIME & DOUBLE TIME
If OT is not 1.5 or 2.0, or DT is not 2.0, a soft inline warning — "unusual — double-check the contract" — appears below the bar and does not block. California agreements are legitimately OT × 2.0.
PREMIUMS EXAMPLE CHIP
Always uses the currently selected wage column's anchor. The multipliers in its ST → OT → DT math are live data, never hardcoded copy.
WAGE COLUMNS
The table shows the selected column's values; derived rows recompute from that column's anchor. Entering Virginia means typing its anchor ($44.44) and the few absolute rates — every derived row fills itself. A new column must be named before Continue is enabled (double-click a pill to rename). With a single wage column, the selection pills are hidden — the "Wage column:" label and "+ Add wage column" stay.
RATE BOOKS
With a single rate book, no book-header rows appear. A simple agreement looks exactly like the current screenshot minus the footer premiums bar.
ADVANCING TO STEP 4
The user can't advance to Step 4 until every wage column is named and all wage data is entered — each rate cell must hold either a value or TBD. (In this prototype a left-blank editable cell already reads as TBD.)
