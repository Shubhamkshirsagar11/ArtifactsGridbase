Routing rules (computed view)
A WO appears in one of three Billing tab buckets at any moment:
  • Ready to Invoice ← status = Complete AND completed_at ≤ cutoff AND not yet invoiced
  • WIP · This Week ← status = In Progress AND has un-accrued earned lines in the current open week
  • WIP · Ready to accrue ← any un-accrued earned lines whose logged_at ≤ last closed week-end (rolling backlog; includes In-Progress WOs and Complete WOs that finished after a back period's close)
Mark as invoiced / accrued are the two write actions that move records into the column 4 ledgers.

Flow paths
1
Job → Contract → CU Library hydrate the production form
When a crew opens a work order to log units, the WO's job_id → contract_id chain resolves to a CU Library. The library's unit_price and description are snapshotted onto each new line at log time. Future price changes don't disturb history.
2
Production lines roll up into their parent work order
WO.lines is the inverse of work_order_id FKs. WO.total is the sum of all billable extended_amounts. Each line carries an immutable logged_at that decides which period it belongs to.
3
WO status + contract rules + period filter route a WO to a bucket
For billing_trigger = per-WO completion (the v1 default): a Complete WO with completed_at ≤ cutoff appears in Ready to Invoice. An In-Progress WO with un-accrued earned lines in any closed week appears in WIP · Ready to accrue. A Complete WO that finished after a back period's close but had earned lines inside it also surfaces under Ready to accrue until its accruals are stamped.
4
Mark as accrued splits earned value into per-week entries
Company-level operational_week_ends_on defines the bucket boundary. Each billable un-accrued line in the WO's "Ready to accrue" view groups into its natural Sun–Sat week; one Accrual entry per week is emitted with accrued_through set to that week's end. source_line_ids[] preserves which lines composed each entry so accounting can drill into the audit trail.
5
Mark as invoiced auto-reverses every open accrual for that WO
The new Invoice record's reverses_accruals[] is populated with every Accrual entry where work_order_id matches AND reversed_by_invoice_id IS NULL. Those accruals get their reversal fields stamped at the same time (reversed_by_invoice_id = invoice.id, reversed_on = today). Net accrued on the books drops; the ledger row stays for audit.
6
All period filtering descends from one company setting
"This week" toggles on Invoiced and Accrued resolve their window from Company.operational_week_ends_on + today. Ready-to-Invoice cutoffs resolve the same way. Period itself is not stored — it's a derived view.
Invariants & edge cases the schema must hold
Net accrued = Σ(accruals where reversed IS NULL)
The "Net accrued on the books" footer on the Accrued ledger is computed from un-reversed entries only. Reversed entries stay visible (grayed) for audit but don't count toward the open balance.
Lines snapshot is immutable on invoice / accrual write
Invoice.lines_snapshot and Accrual.source_line_ids preserve the line state at the moment the action fired. Editing a production log line after the fact must not alter past invoices or accruals — only the live WO total.
Same WO can have multiple accruals but at most one invoice
Per the v1 per-WO billing model, each WO invoices exactly once and that event reverses all open accruals. If billing_trigger ever becomes "partial / progress" (currently Coming Soon), the Invoice → WO relationship widens to many-to-one and the reversal logic gets a period scope.
WIP earned for a period = Σ(lines in period) − Σ(open accruals in period)
After a WO has been accrued for a given week, the WIP row's "Earned this period" must net out the prior accrual so the biller doesn't double-stamp. Once an accrual is reversed by an invoice, the underlying lines become available to re-accrue (relevant only if the invoice itself is later voided).
Closed-period writes are still allowed in v1
Company.enforce_close_lock is display-only for launch (the Period Closed badge is informational). Field crews can still log to closed periods until the toggle ships; the rolling backlog model assumes this.
Currency, time zone, and DOW math live on Company
Every "what week is this?" computation must read Company.operational_week_ends_on + the company TZ. Hardcoding Sun–Sat anywhere in product code will break for tenants that flip to Mon–Sun.
